# Unit Economics & Math

If we deploy this tomorrow, the math is incredibly compelling because the audit is fundamentally a lead-generation layer. We aren't charging for the calculator; we are using it to qualify startups.

### What is a converted lead worth?
Here is how the monetization flows: A startup completes an audit, we identify inefficient AI spend, and then Credex steps in to offer centralized procurement, financing, or optimization services, consolidating their AI purchasing through us.

Let's assume the average startup spends $2,000–$8,000/month on AI tooling, and Credex captures a 5–10% margin or financing spread. 
If a startup routes just $4,000/month of AI tooling through us at a 7% effective margin:
$4,000 × 0.07 = $280/month in margin.
Annualized, that’s **$3,360/year**.

Conservatively, a qualified consultation lead is worth $250–$500 to us, and a fully converted managed account holds a $3,000–$10,000 annual value.

### Estimated CAC By Channel
Our distribution is highly targeted, so our Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) is exceptionally low:
* **Founder Communities / Reddit:** $10–$40 per qualified lead. The cost here is purely sweat equity—time, content creation, and manual outreach. 
* **Public Report Sharing:** <$10 for highly qualified inbound. If reports organically spread between engineering managers and founders, acquisition cost approaches zero (minus server API costs).
* **Direct Founder Outreach:** $50–$150. Manual outreach is a bit slower but the quality is elite.

### Funnel Economics
Here is a realistic early funnel:
* 100 completed audits
* 20 consultation requests (20% conversion from audit)
* 5 serious conversations (25% conversion from request)
* 1 converted infrastructure/procurement customer (20% close rate)

If one converted customer brings in $5,000 ARR, then 100 audits generates $5,000 in ARR. Since the infrastructure and API cost per audit is only $1–$2, the economics are massively in our favor.

### The Path To $1M ARR In 18 Months
To hit $1M ARR at a $5,000 average customer value, we need exactly 200 customers ($1,000,000 ÷ $5,000).

Assuming a conservative 2% overall visitor-to-customer conversion rate, we need 10,000 high-intent users (200 ÷ 0.02) over the next 18 months. That breaks down to just **~550 targeted users a month**. 

This is incredibly realistic assuming public report sharing compounds, engineering communities adopt the tool, AI spend keeps growing, and our benchmarking data becomes more valuable. The biggest assumption isn't traffic—it's that AI spend becomes operationally painful enough for startups to actively seek procurement support. 

### Why The Economics Work So Well
The best economic property of this tool is that the expensive "intelligence" layer is actually tiny. The deterministic audit engine does almost all the heavy math without spending a single LLM token. The AI layer only kicks in at the very end to summarize already-validated outputs. 

This means we have incredibly low inference costs, huge gross margins, predictable scaling, and low operational complexity. It acts as a lightweight, hyper-efficient lead engine rather than a bloated, expensive AI application.
